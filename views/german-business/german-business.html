When doing business in Germany, it is never OK to be late. Germans value "puenktlichkeit" and it is also inconvenient to show up too early. Make sure you are no more than 5-7 minutes early for any appointment unless otherwise instructed, and even two minutes late is a major faux pas.

When meeting a German associate, it is polite to say, "Schoen, Ihnen kennenzulearnen." This means, "Nice to meet you." (Note that this is the formal, polite form of the second person singular). Your counterpart may reply, "Ebenso," which in this context means "Likewise."

Key Vocabulary and Phrases

  • "Danke." Thank you.
  • "Schoen, Ihnen kennenzulernen." Nice to meet you.
  • "Ich habe eienen Termin." I have an appointment.
  • "Danke, dass Sie mir zuhoeren." Thank you for listening to me.
